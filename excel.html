Excel File

In my second year of Kinesiology I took KNES 381, Computer Application in Kinesiology. This class taught me many valuable skills when it comes to technical skills used in data analysis and presentation of kinesiology content. In this course on of the most valuable lessons I learned was how to properly use excel. I learned to navigate excel efficiently, and organize, analyze and visualize kinesiology based datasets. This class emphasized interpretating the skills we have learned and applying them to real-life scenarios. Below is a graph we made in class, we made the pivot chart as seen in Figre 1., a slicer and a pivot table analysis that changes the chart depending on the variables from the pivot table and slicer. By learning to navigate and effectively utilze excel in my second year of university I am confident I will be able to analyze datasets given from lab, or class. Excel is an extremely useful toot and I am glad to have learned more about some of the features it has.

Description of Graph

This graph displays average performance metrics varying form all different trainign years, including VO2max, power output, injury risk, and fatigue. As training experience increase, we see an improvement in VO2max and power output, while injury risk and fatigue levels show more variation. This highlights how long-term training can enhance performance, and emphasizing that it is still important to manage fatigue and injury risk.
